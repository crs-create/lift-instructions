Cabinet Lift
Adjustment Guide

This guide details the operation and fine-tuning of your pneumatic lift to ensure a perfectly flush sewing surface.

Required Tools

8mm Wrench or Hex Driver Measuring Tape (Optional)
Universal Fit
ALL MODELS
STEP
01

Push-to-Release Mechanism

Your cabinet features a three-position air lift: Storage, Sewing, and Flush. The mechanism relies on a "push-to-click" latch system.

OPERATION: RAISE

Push down firmly until you hear a mechanical click, then release completely.

OPERATION: LOWER

Push down until click. Allow mechanism to rise slightly (disengage), then push down freely.

Watch Demo
STEP
02
Important Note

The black positioning block controls the "Center" stop height.

Precision Leveling

Your goal is to make the machine bed perfectly flush with the cabinet top in the "Center" position.

  • 1
    Diagnosis Check Place machine in Center Position and position acrylic insert around it. Check if the machine bed is too high or too low.
  • 2
    Access Mechanism Remove acrylic insert and move lift to the Highest Position (top lock) to expose the adjustment block.
  • 3
    Loosen & Adjust Loosen the two 8mm nuts on the black block. Do not remove.
    Machine too LOW? → Slide block UP
    Machine too HIGH? → Slide block DOWN
  • 4
    Verify Tighten nuts securely. Return to Center Position to verify flushness. Repeat if needed.
Pro Tip: Small adjustments make a big difference. Use your acrylic insert as the ultimate guide for perfect alignment.
STEP
03

Insert Installation

Move lift to the middle position. Fit the custom acrylic insert over the free arm.

Warning

Remove accessory trays from your machine before installing the insert. The insert is precision-cut for the machine's free arm only.

STEP
04

Storage Mode

Convert your cabinet into a flat workspace.

  • A
    Remove Insert Remove the custom acrylic insert from the cabinet.
  • B
    Release Lock Press down firmly on the machine to release the lock.
  • C
    Disengage Allow the lift to rise slightly to disengage the mechanism.
  • D
    Lower Machine Push fully down until it clicks into the bottom storage position.
